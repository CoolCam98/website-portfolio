Who I am

Rooted in craft,
driven by community.

Hey, I'm Camryn. I'm a web developer in Plant City, FL, and I build websites for local businesses. That's the short version. The longer version is that I really, genuinely care about the people I work with and the businesses they're building.

I think most people have had the experience of paying for a website and then never hearing from their developer again. That's not how I work. I stick around. I'm constantly tweaking, improving, watching what's working and what's not. SEO is a huge part of what I do, and I take it seriously from both sides: the technical stuff like structured data and site architecture, and the human side like understanding what real people are actually searching for. I want my clients showing up where it matters.

I'm also really passionate about accessibility. I build every site to WCAG AA standards, not because it checks a box, but because the internet should work for everyone. A more accessible world is just a better world, full stop.

When I'm not building for clients, I'm working on my own stuff. I run a custom LED night light business, I do fun side projects like themed party invitation sites, and I spend a lot of time outside with my plants and bees. Basically, I like making things grow, whether that's a business, a garden, or a weird idea I had at 2 a.m.

Selected work

Things I've made.

01
505 Party
Just for fun
Visit site

This one was just for fun. We love hosting parties, and for this one we went all out with a retro theme. Guests got physical mailed invitations with a password hint ("who lives in a pineapple under the sea?"), and they had to answer it on the site to unlock the party details. Retro games, themed food, the whole thing.

What I did
Built the whole site around the retro concept. The password gate, the reveal, the aesthetic. I wanted the website to feel like part of the party itself, not just a page with an address on it. The mailed invite and the site were designed to work together as one experience.
02
LED 62 — Lumi Lights
My baby
Visit site

This is my own business. I design and make custom acrylic LED night lights (Lumi Lights) and sell them at comic conventions and online. The site needed to feel the way the lights feel: dreamy, soft, a little anime-inspired. Like something from another world.

What I did
Everything. Design, development, e-commerce through Square, product photography direction, all the product copy, and ongoing performance work like image compression and Lighthouse optimization. This one is personal, so it gets the most attention.
03
Southern Heat & AC
Local love
Visit site

A local HVAC company that needed to actually show up when people search for AC repair in their area. The client had a strong color vision I wasn't sure about at first, but once it came together I loved it. This project is a great example of what SEO can do — you're not paying for ads, you're investing in showing up organically.

What I did
Built the full site with accessibility baked in from the start. The big win here is SEO — we got them ranking for "AC repair" in their area and it's driven a real boost in call volume. Built out local SEO landing pages, handled structured data and indexing issues, and set the client up with a Looker Studio dashboard so he can track performance without digging through Search Console. I'm still actively maintaining and improving this one.
